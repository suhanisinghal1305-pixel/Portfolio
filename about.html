The Architect

Suhani Singhal

I am a second year B.Arch student at O.P Jindal Global University, exploring architecture through memory, material, climate, and culture. My interest in design began long before I understood technical drawings or architectural theory. I was fascinated by how spaces could shape emotion, routine, and everyday experience. I am deeply drawn to architecture that responds to its context, buildings that belong to the land they stand on. Through my work, I aim to explore how traditional knowledge, local materials, climate sensitivity, and cultural identity can continue to shape contemporary architecture in meaningful ways.

I believe architecture should do more than function or appear visually appealing. It should communicate. It should hold memory, create atmosphere, and reflect the people and place it is built for. My process is rooted in observation, storytelling, and an ongoing curiosity about how spaces influence human experience.

As I continue learning and evolving as a designer, I hope to create architecture that feels thoughtful, grounded, and emotionally connected to its surroundings.
