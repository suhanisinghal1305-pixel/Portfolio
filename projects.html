Projects

Course - Forensic | Semester - 2

Jsaa Documentation

This project involved documenting the architecture building through on-site measurement and spatial analysis. The building was carefully measured to record accurate dimensions of rooms, circulation areas, roof structure, and architectural elements. Based on these measurements, detailed plans and digital models were developed to represent the building accurately.

Course - Practicum | Semester - 3

Historic Bazaars of Sonipat

This project focused on documenting the historical bazaar of Sonipat through on-site observation, mapping, and community interaction. As part of the practicum, we studied the spatial character of the market by recording shop typologies, building heights, circulation patterns, daily activities, and the relationship between commerce and public life.

Course - Staff and Space | Semester - 3

Multi Generational House

This project presents the design of a residence for a joint family of sixteen members. The aim was to create a home that supports both shared family living and private spaces for each unit. The design focuses on natural light, ventilation, and the integration of greenery within the house.

Course - Staff and Space | Semester - 3

Script to Space

This project explores the relationship between objects and spatial layout by interpreting a script describing three distinct bedrooms. Based on textual clues, the spaces were analyzed and translated into visual collages, layout plans, and a 3D model.

Course - Graphic and Basics | Semester - 1

Exploring Yourself through Art

This project explored self-expression through visual art, where the task was to represent personal identity and emotions through a painting. The aim was to communicate thoughts, experiences, and personal symbolism using artistic elements such as color, texture, and composition.
